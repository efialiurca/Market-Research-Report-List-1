<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Residential buildings occupancy sensors enhance energy efficiency by detecting presence and motion. PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors sense body heat, effectively monitoring space occupancy through infrared radiation. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to detect movement, offering high sensitivity across large areas. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies, reducing false triggers and improving accuracy. Other types may include microwave sensors, which utilize electromagnetic waves, and sensor networks that integrate with smart home systems, providing varied solutions for occupancy detection.</p></p>
