<p><p>The loan servicing market comprises various types of loans catering to different borrower needs. Conventional loans are not insured or guaranteed by government entities and typically require higher credit scores. Conforming loans meet specific criteria set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. FHA loans are government-backed, designed for low-to-moderate-income borrowers. Private money loans are funded by private investors, often with less stringent requirements. Hard money loans are short-term, asset-based loans typically used for investment properties, focusing on collateral rather than creditworthiness.</p></p>
