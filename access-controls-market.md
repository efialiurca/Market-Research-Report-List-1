<p><p>Access control markets can be categorized into card-based and biometrics systems. Card-based systems utilize physical cards, often embedded with magnetic strips or RFID chips, to grant access to restricted areas, relying on user authentication through swiping or tapping. In contrast, biometric systems provide advanced security by identifying individuals based on unique physical attributes, such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris scans. While card-based systems are widely used for their convenience, biometrics offer enhanced security by ensuring that access is granted only to authorized personnel.</p></p>
