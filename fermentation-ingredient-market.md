<p><p>The fermentation ingredient market can be divided into two main types: batch and continuous. Batch fermentation involves processing ingredients in discrete batches, allowing for flexibility and control over the fermentation process. This method is ideal for smaller operations and specialty products. In contrast, continuous fermentation maintains a constant flow of ingredients and products, maximizing efficiency and production capacity. Continuous systems are typically used for large-scale production, providing a steady supply of fermented ingredients while reducing downtime and operational costs.</p></p>
