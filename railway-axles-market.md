Solid axles are robust, typically made from high-strength materials, providing excellent durability and strength for heavy loads. They are favored for their simplicity and reliability. Hollow axles, on the other hand, are designed to be lighter while maintaining structural integrity, which enhances fuel efficiency in locomotives. Both types are critical in ensuring safe and efficient railway operations, catering to different performance and weight requirements.</p></p>
