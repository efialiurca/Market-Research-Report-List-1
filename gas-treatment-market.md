<p><p>The gas treatment market comprises two primary types: amines and non-amines. Amines, such as monoethanolamine (MEA) and diethanolamine (DEA), are widely used for capturing carbon dioxide and hydrogen sulfide from natural gas and biogas, due to their effectiveness and selectivity. Non-amines include solvent-based technologies, physical absorption, and membrane separation, offering alternatives for specific applications where amines may be less efficient or unsuitable. Both categories are essential for environmental compliance and enhancing gas purity in various industrial processes.</p></p>
